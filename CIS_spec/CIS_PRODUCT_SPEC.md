# CIS — Cognitive Intelligence System
## Product Specification

---

## What This Is

CIS is a structured cognitive intelligence system for complex investigation. It manages observations through a defined lifecycle, detects structural patterns across independent information domains, generates and evaluates hypotheses about shared causes, and produces auditable cognitive briefings.

It implements the Earth Edition constraint architecture operationalised through four components:

- **SLS** — Signal Lifecycle System: manages signals from observation through resolution
- **SHG** — Structural Hypothesis Generation: detects cross-domain signal correspondence and generates HCL hypotheses
- **Reasoning** — hypothesis evaluation, plausibility revision, competition management
- **AP-v1** — seven protective constructs (CR, WSP, CDA, SI, HCL, LD, FA) governing what happens to signals at each lifecycle point

---

## Who Uses This

Investigators, analysts, safety reviewers, fraud examiners, intelligence analysts, medical case consultants — anyone conducting structured investigation across multiple information domains where:
- The explanation requires synthesising information from independent sources
- Premature closure is a documented failure risk
- The investigation must be auditable after the fact
- The failure to notice a pattern across individually sub-threshold signals is a known cause of bad outcomes

---

## Core Concepts

**Signal:** A structured observation — any piece of information registered into the investigation. Not just anomalies. A signal can be a reading within normal parameters that is structurally incongruent with its context (SI-bearing) or a direct contradiction of an existing account.

**Signal Pool:** The managed set of all signals in the investigation. Signals are not stored flat — they move through states (Candidate → Admitted → Retained → Connected / Quarantined / Elevated → Assessed → Resolved → Archived).

**Domain:** An independent organisational or technical source. MCO had two domains: AMD navigation and JPL navigation. Therac-25 had four: each independent hospital. Domains are the structural units for cross-domain analysis.

**SI score:** A composite score of a signal's structural incongruence — how much its structural properties deviate from what its classified context predicts. Four dimensions: rate of change, direction, cross-dimension relationship, constraint configuration. SI_min = 0.25 for Signal Pool admission.

**Loss points:** Seven specific points where signals are systematically lost in standard investigation (LP-1: threshold exclusion; LP-2: single-period discard; LP-3: domain boundary loss; LP-4: explanation displacement; LP-5: aggregation failure; LP-6: significance decay; LP-7: closure-induced discard). CIS protects against all seven.

**HCL hypothesis:** A structural claim that signals from independent domains share a common structural cause. Generated by SHG when cross-domain signal correspondence exceeds the independence threshold.

**Cognitive briefing:** Structured output produced by the system at any point in the investigation: active signal pool summary, highest-significance retained signals, active hypotheses with plausibility, quarantined contradictions, resolved findings, loss points flagged.

## What It Does

### Signal Intake and Admission

Investigator submits an observation. System scores it on four SI dimensions and computes a composite SI score. If SI ≥ SI_min, a Candidate is created. The Candidate is evaluated against six significance criteria (NL4-B scoring). If significance ≥ SIG_threshold, the signal is Admitted to the Signal Pool. If significance < SIG_threshold but SI ≥ SI_min, the signal enters the Pool at sub-threshold status (retained per WSP).

Every admission decision is recorded in the Admission Audit — what was submitted, how it was scored, why it was admitted or not.

**LP-1 protection:** No observation is discarded before Candidate creation. Everything submitted enters as a Candidate. Rejection happens at the Admission stage with a recorded reason — not before.

### Signal Preservation (AP-v1 WSP)

Admitted signals cannot be expired within the minimum retention period (two observation periods). Sub-threshold signals are aggregated across the pool before expiry consideration — the aggregate pattern is assessed, not individual signals.

### Cross-Domain Handling (AP-v1 CDA / HCL detection)

Each signal is tagged with its source domain. When a signal is admitted, the system checks for structural correspondence with retained signals from independent domains. Correspondence criteria: mismatch type, dimension, temporal pattern, direction. When correspondence is detected, both signals are elevated to Connected state and flagged for SHG.

### Contradiction Handling (AP-v1 CR)

When two signals about the same structural state contradict each other, both enter Quarantine state. Neither can be resolved or expired while in Quarantine. Resolution requires one of:
- RC-1: source calibration invalidated (one signal's source is confirmed unreliable)
- RC-2: falsification (one signal directly predicts an observable condition that is demonstrably absent)
- RC-3: domain resolution (investigator with direct observational access confirms which condition is present)

Every Quarantine state and resolution is recorded in the Contradiction Ledger.

### Hypothesis Generation (SHG)

When connected signals from independent domains accumulate, SHG evaluates whether their correspondence is better explained by a shared structural source than by independent coincidence. When the probability of independent co-occurrence falls below threshold, SHG generates a structural hypothesis naming the candidate shared cause.

Hypothesis types: HCL (hidden common link), SI_CLUSTER (multiple SI-bearing signals from a single domain), PATTERN (temporal accumulation warranting aggregate assessment).

### Reasoning and Plausibility

Each hypothesis has a plausibility score updated as evidence accumulates. Evidence can be Supporting (increases plausibility), Contradicting (decreases plausibility), or Contextual (provides framing without directional pressure). Competing hypotheses are managed in competition sets — increasing the plausibility of one decreases others in the set.

Resolution conditions: plausibility ≥ 0.85 with no active contradicting evidence → CONFIRMED. Plausibility ≤ 0.10 with strong falsifying evidence → FALSIFIED.

### Cognitive Briefing

At any point, the system generates a structured briefing: what is known, what is hypothesised, what is contradicted, what is still open. Briefings are stored and timestamped — the investigation's epistemic state is auditable at every point.

## What It Collects (Research Dataset)

The system collects anonymous investigation quality data across all cases:
- Signal admission rates (% of Candidates admitted)
- Signal loss point distribution (where do signals exit the pool)
- Assumption closure rate (% of commitments by assumption vs direct test)
- Cross-domain connection rate (% of investigations with connected signals)
- HCL hypothesis generation rate
- HCL hypothesis confirmation rate
- Contradiction quarantine frequency and resolution basis distribution
- Option hit rate (released options that eventually match)
- Cognitive briefing utilisation

No case content. No identifying information. Aggregate patterns only.

---

## What It Does Not Do

- Reach conclusions. The system surfaces patterns and generates hypotheses. Investigators assess them.
- Replace domain expertise. SI scoring, hypothesis plausibility, and resolution assessment all require human judgment.
- Store personal data. Cases are anonymous. No accounts required.
