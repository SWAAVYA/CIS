# Case T1-002: Mars Climate Orbiter

**Case ID:** T1-002  
**Pre-Decision Phase scope:** TCM-1 execution (January 18, 1999) → Navigation periapsis estimate of approximately 170 km (September 15, 1999)  
**Decision Phase:** Mars Orbit Insertion burn, September 23, 1999  
**Primary failure mode designation (M-06):** CDA — cross-domain signal integration failure  
**Domain:** Aerospace / Software Engineering / Mission Operations

---

## T1-002 — A1: Predicted EE Structures

### 1. Hidden Common Link (HCL) — PRIMARY PREDICTION

**Prediction:** The Pre-Decision Phase evidence environment will contain at least two signals from structurally independent sources — one from the spacecraft operations domain (AMD Micropropulsion navigation outputs) and one from the mission navigation domain (mission navigation trajectory computations) — that share a common underlying cause not identified during the decision process: the incompatibility of unit conventions between the two systems.

**Rationale:** The case domain is Aerospace/Software Engineering/Mission Operations with a designated CDA primary failure. HCL is the EE structural pattern most predictive of cross-domain integration failures where a shared cause exists across apparently independent systems. When two organizationally separate systems use incommensurable conventions for the same physical parameter, the relationship between them constitutes a Hidden Common Link: each system's outputs appear valid within its own domain, while the shared architectural incompatibility is invisible from within either domain alone.

The framework predicts HCL-1 (signals from structurally independent sources) as satisfied by the separation between the spacecraft propulsion team and the mission navigation team. HCL-2 (non-connection documented during decision phase) is predicted to be present as the lack of integrated unit-convention verification across the interface. HCL-3 (investigation confirmed shared structural cause) is predicted to be established by the investigation. HCL-4 (connection non-obvious from within either domain) is predicted because unit convention management is typically invisible within each system's operational context.

**EE Structure sub-criteria predicted present:** HCL-1, HCL-2, HCL-3, HCL-4

---

### 2. Load Displacement (LD) — SECONDARY PREDICTION

**Prediction:** The Pre-Decision Phase evidence environment will contain evidence that risk associated with the unit incompatibility was held within the software engineering domain without the information required for the mission navigation team to evaluate or detect it.

**Rationale:** CDA failures characteristically involve Load Displacement because the domain boundary that prevents signal integration also prevents risk awareness transfer. The framework predicts LD-1 (risk transferred without information) as satisfied if the interface between the propulsion software and the navigation computation represents a transfer point at which the units-discrepant output was accepted as valid input. LD-2 (source appears stable after transfer) is predicted because the AMD propulsion subsystem would have functioned correctly within its own operational context. LD-3 (destination holds risk without assessment) is predicted because the navigation team would have been computing on unit-discrepant data without independent verification capacity.

**EE Structure sub-criteria predicted present:** LD-1, LD-2, LD-3

---

### 3. Cascade Precondition (CP) — TERTIARY PREDICTION

**Prediction:** The Pre-Decision Phase evidence environment will contain at least two coupled failure modes — the unit-discrepant data accumulation and the absence of a cross-domain verification mechanism — such that failure in either produces amplified rather than absorbed consequences in the coupled domain.

**Rationale:** Navigation trajectory errors accumulating over nine months through multiple trajectory correction maneuvers constitute a cascade where each corrective action that applied unit-discrepant data reinforced rather than resolved the trajectory divergence. The framework predicts this coupling was not reflected in the MOI risk assessment.

**Note:** A-20 (Clarification Addendum v2) applies to CP coding decisions in reconstruction. Specific A-20 requirements for CP sub-criteria documentation apply to Artifact B coding, not to Artifact A predictions.

**EE Structure sub-criteria predicted present:** CP coupling between trajectory error accumulation and verification absence; absence of coupling in decision-phase risk model predicted.

---

### 4. Fragility Accumulation (FA) — SUPPLEMENTARY PREDICTION

**Prediction:** The Pre-Decision Phase evidence environment may contain evidence that multiple independently managed margins — trajectory position uncertainty, navigation validation coverage, cross-team interface verification — were simultaneously reduced below nominal levels at the time of the Mars Orbit Insertion decision.

**Rationale:** CDA-type failures sometimes involve Fragility Accumulation where the absence of cross-domain integration mechanisms constitutes one of several simultaneously degraded margins. However, FA is less characteristic of CDA failures than HCL or LD. This prediction carries lower confidence.

---

### 5. Threshold Instability (TI) — LOW CONFIDENCE PREDICTION

**Prediction:** The evidence environment may contain evidence that at least one critical navigation parameter was operating within measurement uncertainty of its design limit during the Decision Phase.

**Rationale:** The periapsis estimate that the investigation established was significantly below the safe orbital insertion corridor. Whether this periapsis discrepancy was within measurement uncertainty of the threshold during the Pre-Decision Phase is uncertain without case access.

## T1-002 — A2: Predicted AP Signal Types

### WSP — Weak Signal Preservation

**Prediction:** The Pre-Decision Phase evidence environment will contain individually sub-threshold navigation anomaly signals — individual trajectory deviations that, when reviewed in isolation, did not exceed the threshold for formal mission operations review action.

**Predicted signal characteristics:**
- Individual trajectory correction maneuver output deviations below single-event review threshold
- Periapsis altitude estimates progressively diverging from expected range without triggering formal anomaly review
- Navigation team observations of trajectory uncertainty not formally escalated because individual deviations were within acceptable bands

**Rationale:** WSP failures are predicted when signals are available but individually sub-threshold. The nine-month accumulation of unit-discrepant data through multiple TCMs implies a progressive signal that was visible in aggregate but was not aggregated for review. Each individual TCM deviation would have been assessed individually against individual-event thresholds.

---

### CDA — Cross-Domain Admission

**Prediction — HIGH CONFIDENCE:** The Pre-Decision Phase evidence environment will contain at least one signal that required cross-domain integration — between the spacecraft propulsion software domain and the mission navigation domain — that was not admitted into the decision process.

**Predicted signal characteristics:**
- AMD Micropropulsion Small Forces data output formatted in imperial units, constituting a signal about the thrust history that required interpretation against the navigation team's metric-unit trajectory model
- The unit-discrepant data as a cross-domain signal that was generated in the software engineering domain and entered the navigation domain without domain-boundary translation
- Absence of a cross-domain verification interface as a structural signal that the organizational architecture did not include CDA-satisfying integration mechanisms

**Rationale:** This is the primary AP prediction for T1-002, directly matching the M-06 designation of CDA as the primary failure mode. The framework predicts that the signal requiring cross-domain admission was generated, was available, and was not admitted into the trajectory decision process because the organizational admissibility architecture did not include the interface required to translate between unit conventions across domains.

---

### CR — Contradiction Retention

**Prediction — LOW CONFIDENCE:** The Pre-Decision Phase evidence environment may contain contradictory signals about the navigation trajectory — where one signal indicated nominal progress and another signal, available but not foregrounded, indicated a problematic periapsis trajectory — and that the contradiction was resolved by accepting the nominal signal.

**Rationale:** CR is less characteristic of pure CDA failures than of organizational decision-making failures. For T1-002, the unit discrepancy may have produced contradictory trajectory indications that were resolved by accepting the software output as authoritative. However, this prediction carries low confidence because CR failures typically involve explicit contradiction visible to decision-makers, which is less clearly characteristic of unit-convention failures where the contradiction is architecturally hidden.

## T1-002 — A3: Predicted Visibility Failures

**Predicted primary visibility failure — CDA institutional filtering:**

The framework predicts that the organizational boundary between the AMD Micropropulsion team and the mission navigation team constituted an admissibility gate: information about the unit convention used by the propulsion software was not structurally admitted into the mission navigation trajectory computation workflow. This is predicted to reflect not individual negligence but an architectural gap — the interface specification did not require unit-convention verification at the domain crossing point.

**Predicted secondary visibility failure — WSP aggregation absence:**

Individual trajectory deviations were assessed against single-event thresholds without a mechanism for aggregating the cumulative trajectory error across the nine-month cruise phase. The framework predicts the absence of such a mechanism was the filtering mechanism — not that each individual signal was suppressed, but that no institutional process existed to combine individually acceptable signals into a cumulative-deviation assessment.

**Predicted institutional filtering pattern:** Domain-boundary architectural gap — the organizational structure did not include a verification role or process at the interface between propulsion software and navigation computation. This is a structural prediction about the admissibility architecture, not a prediction about individual decisions.

# Case T1-003: Ariane 5 Flight 501

**Case ID:** T1-003  
**Pre-Decision Phase scope:** Specification and acceptance of SRI incorporating reused Ariane 4 flight software (Ariane 5 development, approximately 1992–1994) → Final launch commit authorization (June 4, 1996, approximately 09:33 UTC)  
**Decision Phase:** Launch of Ariane 5 Flight 501 through vehicle destruction (June 4, 1996, T+0 through T+37 seconds)  
**Primary failure mode designation (M-06):** Prior-model admissibility failure (SI-type) — the reuse assumption operated as a signal-filtering prior model  
**Domain:** Aerospace / Software Engineering

---

## T1-003 — A1: Predicted EE Structures

### 1. Load Displacement (LD) — PRIMARY PREDICTION

**Prediction:** The Pre-Decision Phase evidence environment will contain evidence that the software validation burden — specifically the responsibility to verify that the Ariane 4 IRS software was safe for the Ariane 5 flight trajectory — was displaced from the software engineering domain to the operational assumption that prior validation was sufficient.

**Rationale:** Prior-model SI failures are structurally characterized by Load Displacement where the validation burden that would normally be applied to a new system is transferred to the prior-model assumption ("this software was validated for Ariane 4, therefore it is safe"). This transfer occurs without the information required for the receiving party — flight operations — to evaluate whether the prior model's conditions hold in the new context.

The framework predicts LD-1 (risk transferred without information): the risk of trajectory-profile incompatibility was held within the software engineering decision to reuse without revalidation, and was not transferred with the information the flight operations context would need to detect it. LD-2 (source appears stable): the Ariane 4 software record was operationally stable and validated. LD-3 (destination holds risk without assessment): the Ariane 5 flight operations proceeded without trajectory-specific IRS software validation.

**EE Structure sub-criteria predicted present:** LD-1, LD-2, LD-3

---

### 2. Hidden Common Link (HCL) — PRIMARY PREDICTION

**Prediction:** The Pre-Decision Phase evidence environment will contain at least two apparently independent signals — one about the IRS software behavior and one about the Ariane 5 trajectory profile — that share a common structural cause not identified during the pre-decision phase: the functional incompatibility between the software's representable horizontal velocity range and the Ariane 5 trajectory's actual horizontal velocity.

**Rationale:** The prior-model SI failure produces HCL when the assumption of prior validation connects apparently separate systems. The Ariane 4 software's performance record and the Ariane 5 trajectory specification are from separate engineering domains; their shared link — the dependency of software safety on trajectory-specific parameters — constitutes a Hidden Common Link that was not made explicit in the reuse decision.

HCL-1 (signals from structurally independent sources): Software engineering record vs. trajectory engineering specification. HCL-2 (non-connection documented during decision phase): The reuse decision is predicted to have been made without establishing that the trajectory-software compatibility was verified. HCL-3 (investigation confirmed shared cause): The Inquiry Board established the shared cause. HCL-4 (connection non-obvious from within either domain): The specific parameter dependency was not visible within standard software reuse review procedures.

**EE Structure sub-criteria predicted present:** HCL-1, HCL-2, HCL-3, HCL-4

---

### 3. Cascade Precondition (CP) — SECONDARY PREDICTION

**Prediction:** The Pre-Decision Phase evidence environment will contain evidence of at least two coupled failure modes — IRS software overflow producing erroneous attitude data, and flight computer acceptance of IRS data as valid — such that the failure in the IRS propagated rather than was absorbed by the redundant flight computer system.

**Rationale:** The use of a backup IRS that contains the same software constitutes a cascade structure where both backup and primary IRS were exposed to the same failure mechanism. The coupling between the primary and backup systems through their shared software architecture is a cascade precondition: the failure of one does not trigger compensatory behavior in the other because both fail by the same mechanism.

**Note:** A-20 (Clarification Addendum v2) applies to CP coding decisions in reconstruction. Specifically, the shared-software-architecture coupling predicted here must meet A-20's documented CP sub-criteria requirements in Artifact B.

---

### 4. Threshold Instability (TI) — SECONDARY PREDICTION

**Prediction:** The Pre-Decision Phase evidence environment will contain evidence that the Ariane 5 trajectory profile operated in a horizontal velocity range near or above the representable limit of the IRS software's data type for that parameter.

**Rationale:** The framework predicts TI when a critical parameter operates within measurement uncertainty of a design limit. For T1-003, the design limit is the software's representable range for horizontal velocity, and the trajectory-specific velocity is predicted to be within the region where overflow was possible or likely.

---

### 5. Fragility Accumulation (FA) — LOW CONFIDENCE PREDICTION

**Prediction:** The Pre-Decision Phase evidence environment may contain evidence that multiple independently managed margins — software validation coverage, interface specification completeness, trajectory-specific testing — were simultaneously below their designed levels.

**Rationale:** FA is predicted with low confidence for this case because the failure has a more concentrated structural cause (a single reuse decision) than the multi-margin degradation characteristic of classical FA cases.

# Case T1-004: Space Shuttle Columbia (STS-107)

**Case ID:** T1-004  
**Pre-Decision Phase scope:** First documented foam debris strike on shuttle external tank in Space Shuttle program history → STS-107 foam strike and initial MMT notification (January 16–17, 2003)  
**Decision Phase:** MMT assessment process and decision not to pursue imaging (January 17–24, 2003)  
**Primary failure mode designation (M-06):** WSP + CR — weak signal normalization and contradiction retention failure  
**Domain:** Aerospace / Organizational Decision-Making

---

## T1-004 — A1: Predicted EE Structures

### 1. Fragility Accumulation (FA) — PRIMARY PREDICTION

**Prediction:** The Pre-Decision Phase evidence environment will contain evidence that at least three independently managed system margins — thermal protection system (TPS) robustness, foam debris generation rate, and organizational safety assessment confidence — were simultaneously degraded below their nominal design levels at the time of STS-107.

**Rationale:** WSP failures in organizational decision-making cases are most strongly correlated with FA in the EE structural framework. The normalization of foam debris strikes across multiple prior missions represents the classic FA pattern: each individually classified-as-acceptable anomaly represents a margin degradation, and the accumulation across 87+ missions represents the aggregate fragility state. The framework predicts that FA will be present and documentable from the pre-mission foam debris strike record.

The framework predicts FA-1 (at least three independently managed margins simultaneously degraded): TPS foam debris tolerance margin (degraded by prior strikes), organizational risk assessment confidence margin (degraded by normalization precedent), and safety authority margin (degraded by the decision-making process that routinely classified foam strikes as acceptable). Each margin is managed by a distinct organizational function; each is simultaneously below nominal at STS-107 launch.

**EE Structure sub-criteria predicted present:** FA-1, FA-2, FA-3 (minimum three simultaneously degraded independent margins at time of STS-107)

---

### 2. Load Displacement (LD) — PRIMARY PREDICTION

**Prediction:** The Pre-Decision Phase evidence environment will contain evidence that the technical risk associated with TPS foam debris vulnerability was displaced from the engineering assessment domain to the mission management decision domain without the information required for the mission management domain to evaluate the risk independently.

**Rationale:** WSP + CR failure combinations in organizational cases characteristically involve LD where engineering-domain risk knowledge is transferred to management without the full technical context. The framework predicts: LD-1 — technical risk about foam strike damage severity transferred from engineering to MMT without full damage assessment capability; LD-2 — the engineering domain appears to have resolved the concern by classifying prior strikes as acceptable, making the source domain appear stable; LD-3 — the MMT receives the risk assessment without the imaging or structural analysis information that would enable independent evaluation.

**EE Structure sub-criteria predicted present:** LD-1, LD-2, LD-3

---

### 3. Cascade Precondition (CP) — SECONDARY PREDICTION

**Prediction:** The Pre-Decision Phase evidence environment will contain evidence of at least two coupled failure modes — TPS damage from foam strike and the decision not to pursue imaging assessment — such that the absence of imaging created conditions for the failure to progress undetected rather than being identified as a cascade precondition.

**Rationale:** The coupling between TPS damage (structural) and imaging decision (organizational/informational) constitutes a cascade where the absence of information about the structural failure enabled the structural failure to progress. The framework predicts this coupling was not reflected in the mission management risk model.

**Note:** A-20 (Clarification Addendum v2) applies to CP coding decisions in reconstruction.

---

### 4. Hidden Common Link (HCL) — SECONDARY PREDICTION

**Prediction:** The Pre-Decision Phase evidence environment will contain at least two apparently independent signals — the foam debris strike history across multiple missions and the STS-107 ascent foam strike — that share a common structural cause not identified in the MMT decision process: the systematic underestimation of foam debris impact energy and TPS damage potential across all prior missions.

**Rationale:** The normalization pattern across 87+ missions creates HCL when the common structural cause of all prior acceptable-damage classifications — the underestimation model — also applied to the STS-107 classification. HCL is satisfied if the investigation establishes a shared structural cause (the risk model) connecting apparently independent damage events across missions.

## T1-004 — A3: Predicted Visibility Failures

**Predicted primary visibility failure — WSP normalization mechanism:**

The framework predicts that the mechanism filtering individual foam debris strike signals from aggregation into a pattern-level concern was the normalization precedent established across prior missions. Each acceptable classification created a precedent that lowered the effective signal threshold for subsequent missions. This is a predicted institutional filtering pattern: the accumulation of acceptable classifications created a self-reinforcing admissibility architecture in which the relevant question was "is this strike within the range of what we have previously accepted?" rather than "what is the actual probability that this strike has caused unacceptable TPS damage?"

**Predicted secondary visibility failure — CR resolution mechanism:**

The framework predicts that the contradiction between the imaging request (signal: uncertainty is high enough to warrant independent assessment) and the MMT dismissal (signal: existing data is sufficient to characterize the risk as acceptable) was resolved through an organizational authority structure in which the mission management role held the authority to characterize risk, and the engineering concern was a subordinate input. The visibility failure is the mechanism by which the CR-satisfying contradiction was present but resolved before it entered the decision record as an unresolved uncertainty.

**Predicted institutional filtering pattern:** Normalization-through-precedent — the classification history creates a de facto admissibility threshold for foam strikes that filters signals about actual damage uncertainty. This is a structural prediction about the organizational admissibility architecture built up across the shuttle program, not a prediction about STS-107 decisions in isolation.

## T1-004 — A4: Predicted Alternative Explanations

**Alternative explanation 1 — Organizational culture:**
The CAIB investigation is expected to have identified organizational culture — including the normalization of deviance and the cultural disposition to accept engineering risk without fully characterizing it — as a causal factor. The alternative explanation: Columbia was lost because of an organizational culture that did not appropriately weight engineering concern. This is a systemic account that does not require EE/CIS structural vocabulary.

**Alternative explanation 2 — Technical classification error:**
The evidence may support an account that the foam strike damage assessment was technically incorrect — that with available tools and methods, the strike should have been assessed as exceeding safe limits. Alternative: Columbia was lost because the MMT made a technical error in assessing the foam strike risk.

**Alternative explanation 3 — Communication failure:**
The evidence may support an account that engineering concern about the foam strike was not effectively communicated to the MMT. Alternative: Columbia was lost because engineers did not communicate their uncertainty clearly enough for management to act on it. This is a process failure account rather than a structural EE account.

**Alternative explanation 4 — Resource constraint:**
The evidence may support an account that imaging resources (satellite imagery or shuttle remote arm inspection) were not available or not requested for institutional reasons unrelated to the technical risk assessment. This would be an alternative proximate explanation for why imaging was not pursued.

## T1-004 — A6: Falsification Conditions

**For Fragility Accumulation:**
This prediction is disconfirmed if the reconstruction finds: (a) fewer than three independently managed margins were simultaneously below nominal at STS-107 launch, OR (b) the foam debris strike history across prior missions did not constitute a documentable series of margin degradations — specifically, if the prior strikes were within fully nominal design limits rather than at the boundary of acceptable performance.

**For Load Displacement:**
This prediction is disconfirmed if the reconstruction finds: (a) the MMT had access to a structural damage assessment that provided sufficient information to evaluate the TPS risk independently without requesting imaging, OR (b) the technical risk characterization by engineering fully equipped the MMT to evaluate the risk within the MMT's own competency.

**For WSP:**
This prediction is disconfirmed if the reconstruction finds no individual foam debris strike records in the pre-STS-107 mission history that were each classified as acceptable without aggregation — i.e., if each prior foam strike either was individually above the concern threshold or was assessed in aggregate with others.

**For CR:**
This prediction is disconfirmed if the reconstruction finds: (a) no signal from the engineering domain indicated that the STS-107 foam strike damage was uncertain or potentially significant, OR (b) the contradiction between engineering uncertainty and the MMT's nominal characterization was explicitly recognized and formally resolved through a documented risk-acceptance process that included the uncertainty range.
