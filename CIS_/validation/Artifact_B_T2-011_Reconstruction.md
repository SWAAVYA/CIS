## Signal Inventory

### Pre-Decision Phase Signals

**PRE-001:** IFE system modification and certification — the MD-11 aircraft operated by Swissair were fitted with an in-flight entertainment system that used wiring insulated with MPET (Metallized Polyethylene Terephthalate), a polyethylene-based material. MPET was approved under certification testing that did not replicate the specific thermal environment of the MD-11 overhead area during continuous IFE operation. The material's flammability characteristics under in-situ installation conditions — where heat could accumulate in the enclosed overhead space — were not fully characterized. The certification approved the material based on standard flammability tests that did not match the operational installation geometry and thermal environment.

- **SI-1:** PRESENT HIGH — the certification specification assumed that standard flammability test results were valid for the in-situ installation environment. The assumption was structurally incorrect: MPET's flammability behavior in an enclosed overhead space with accumulated heat differed from its behavior under standard open test conditions.
- **LD:** PRESENT HIGH — the risk that MPET's in-situ flammability differed from standard test results was accepted in the certification domain and displaced to the operational domain. Airlines and crews operating the MD-11 with this IFE system had no capacity to evaluate or mitigate MPET flammability once airborne.

**PRE-002:** CDA assessment — the materials characterization gap (MPET flammability in enclosed overhead installation) existed within the certification testing literature. This characterization was not admitted into the IFE modification certification decision in a form that required resolution. The specific combination of material + installation geometry + thermal environment under continuous operation was not tested. PRESENT MODERATE.

**CR assessment — Pre-Decision Phase:**

The SUST report documents a certification process gap rather than a documented contradiction closed by organizational pressure. The investigation does not identify a specific instance where a concern about MPET flammability in the MD-11 overhead installation was raised by a named party, reviewed, and closed as acceptable without direct testing. The certification gap was systemic — the testing methodology did not cover the relevant scenario — rather than a case of a raised concern being suppressed.

**→ CR ABSENT in Pre-Decision Phase.** The certification failure is SI-1 and LD, not CR. No contradicting signal is documented as raised and resolved without direct disconfirmation.

### Decision Phase Signals

**DEC-001:** September 3, 1998, approximately 01:10 UTC — Crew detects smell and then visible smoke in the cockpit. They declare emergency and begin diverting to Halifax. Checklist execution begins.

**DEC-002:** 01:14–01:31 UTC — The fire, propagating through the overhead IFE wiring, progresses rapidly. Electrical systems begin failing. The aircraft crashes into the ocean before reaching Halifax.

The decision phase is rapid and the crew responds correctly — they identify the problem and take action. The failure is not in crew decision-making during the emergency; it is in the structural conditions established during the pre-decision phase (SI-1, LD) that made the in-flight fire possible.
