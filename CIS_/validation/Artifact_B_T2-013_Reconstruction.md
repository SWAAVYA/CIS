# Artifact B — T2-013 Aloha Airlines Flight 243

**Status:** FROZEN | **Date:** 2026-06-02 | **Artifact A:** SEALED  
**Sources:** NTSB AAR-89/03 (1989)

## Construct Determinations

**WSP — PRESENT | HIGH:** Each of N73711's 89,680 flights contributed sub-threshold fatigue damage. Each maintenance inspection found the aircraft within acceptable limits. The aggregate pattern — exceptional cycle accumulation producing multi-site fatigue damage — was never formally assessed as requiring modified inspection intervals. The pattern was visible in cycle count records; no mechanism translated it into enhanced inspection. WSP is the primary construct.

**SI-1 — PRESENT | MODERATE:** 

**This disconfirms the prediction of ABSENT HIGH.**

The judgment rests on the verification test: did the inspection specification embed an assumption about adjacent system behaviour that was practicably verifiable and was not verified?

The NTSB found that the inspection programme relied on visual and eddy current inspection methods calibrated for detecting single fatigue cracks growing from discrete initiation sites. Multi-site damage — numerous small cracks coalescing across a wide area of the lap joint — is a qualitatively different failure mode. The inspection specification embedded an assumption that its detection methods were adequate for the failure modes present in a 737 at extreme cycle accumulation. This assumption was not verified for multi-site damage in an aircraft approaching or exceeding the DSO. The adjacent system — the fatigue damage state of N73711's structure at 89,680 cycles — behaved differently from what the inspection methodology assumed.

The distinction from pure LD: the LD argument (operator applied the specification beyond its scope) is also present. But the SI-1 condition is real and independent of the operator's decision: the inspection specification's assumption about detection adequacy was not verified for multi-site fatigue damage as a failure mode, even within the DSO. An aircraft at 55,000 cycles with multi-site damage would also have been missed by the inspection programme — the detection adequacy assumption is not just a post-DSO problem.

SI-1 PRESENT at MODERATE — the detection adequacy assumption was embedded in the inspection specification and was not verified for the multi-site failure mode. This is the disconfirmation.

**LD — PRESENT | MODERATE:** Aloha's continued operation of N73711 far beyond the DSO without enhanced inspection or manufacturer guidance. Post-DSO risk accepted without adequate evaluation capacity. Confirmed.

**FA — PRESENT | MODERATE:** High cycle accumulation rate from short-haul operations; absence of enhanced post-DSO inspection programme; inspection methodology limitation for multi-site damage; no manufacturer alert for N73711's exceptional service life. Multiple simultaneously degraded conditions. Confirmed.

**CR — ABSENT | HIGH:** No documented case of a concern about N73711's structural integrity raised and closed without direct test. The fatigue state was not generating visible signals. Confirmed absent.

**HCL, CDA — ABSENT | HIGH:** Confirmed absent.
