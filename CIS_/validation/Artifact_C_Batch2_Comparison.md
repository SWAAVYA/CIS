## Comparison Methodology

Classifications follow the Artifact C Batch 1 standard:

- **CONFIRMED:** Artifact B finding confirms the Artifact A prediction at the level predicted; sub-criteria match.
- **PARTIAL:** Artifact B partially supports the prediction — structure found but at lower confidence, fewer sub-criteria, or in a different form than predicted.
- **NOT CONFIRMED:** Artifact B contradicts the prediction or codes the structure Absent.

**FCR = Confirmed Predictions / Total Valid Predictions.** Partial confirmations excluded from the numerator. No Evidence-Limited predictions identified.

### Constructs Requiring Refinement

**TI (Threshold Instability):** 5 CONFIRMED + 1 PARTIAL (T2-003) across 6 cases. T2-003 reveals a boundary condition: when a system fails by definitively crossing a threshold (binary activation failure) rather than operating within measurement uncertainty of a threshold, TI's applicability is weaker. TI is most reliably applicable when the system is operating near a continuous parameter threshold; it is weaker for binary-activation failures. This distinction should be incorporated into the Clarification Addendum v3.

**SI (Structural Incongruence):** Found in all 6 cases (supplementary) but outside the FCR vocabulary due to GD-006. The consistent finding across organizational, software, and manufacturing domains makes SI the program's most significant vocabulary gap. T2-003's hardware-embedded SI (a new SI instantiation type) extends the structural type beyond the prior cases' organizational or specification-level incongruences. GD-006 Condition C (Program Vocabulary Review) should be initiated immediately.
