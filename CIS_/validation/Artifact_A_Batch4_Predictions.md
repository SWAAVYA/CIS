## Case T2-006 — X-33 Composite LOX Tank Failure

**CASE-ID:** T2-006  
**Tier:** 2 | **AI Prior Exposure:** MEDIUM-LOW  
**Outcome:** The X-33 reusable launch vehicle demonstrator's composite liquid oxygen tank failed during a ground pressure test in November 1999, causing the inner tank wall to separate from the honeycomb core under combined cryogenic loading. The failure ended the X-33 program.

**Phase Boundaries:**
- Pre-Decision Phase start: Composite LOX tank material specification and design acceptance — X-33 development phase, approximately 1996–1997
- Pre-Decision Phase end: Final ground pressure test configuration, November 1999
- Decision Phase: Cryogenic pressure test sequence
- Post-Decision Phase end: Tank structural failure

**Falsification conditions stated before reconstruction:**
- SI-2 fails if the reconstruction shows that the cryogenic combined-load performance of the composite material was formally verified at the design-to-structures boundary before the tank was accepted into the vehicle design
- CR fails if no documented concern about composite cryogenics was raised and closed during the development program
- LD fails if the composite technology risk was characterized as mature at program inception rather than transferred from a development domain

---

### CR — PRESENT | HIGH

The X-33 composite LOX tank used a bismaleimide composite material system. The behavior of composite laminates under combined cryogenic and mechanical loading was not fully characterized for the specific geometry and load profile of the X-33 tank at the time of design acceptance. Engineering concerns about composite microcracking under cryogenic cycling were raised during the development program. The concerns were assessed as manageable within existing design margins and the program continued on schedule. The closure mechanism: alternative explanation adoption (existing data plus analytical models are sufficient) rather than direct test of the specific combined loading condition at full scale. Options Released: whether the composite laminate would maintain structural integrity under the actual combined cryogenic and pressurization loading of the X-33 tank geometry.

**Prediction: PRESENT HIGH**

---

### SI — PRESENT | HIGH | SI-2

The composite material characterization data was generated by the materials team under controlled coupon-level test conditions. This data crossed the boundary into the structural design team's analysis as material property inputs. The boundary crossing did not include verification that coupon-level cryogenic properties were valid for the full-scale tank's structural geometry, manufacturing process variations, and combined loading profile. This is an SI-2 condition: information (material properties) crossed an organizational and technical boundary (from materials characterization to structural design) without adequate structural verification that the crossing preserved the information's validity for the receiving context. Coupon-level cryogenic performance does not automatically translate to system-level cryogenic performance in composite honeycomb sandwich panels under combined loading.

**Prediction: PRESENT HIGH | Sub-criterion: SI-2**

This prediction is falsifiable. If the reconstruction shows a formal coupon-to-system verification protocol that specifically addressed the combined cryogenic and mechanical loading condition at the design boundary, SI-2 is absent.

---

### LD — PRESENT | HIGH

The composite LOX tank technology represented a significant advancement beyond prior flight-validated composite applications. The X-33 program accepted this technology from the research and development domain into a flight vehicle program. The risk that composite cryogenic behavior at system scale differed from coupon-level characterization was transferred from the R&D domain (where continued characterization would have been the appropriate response) to the vehicle program (where schedule and cost constraints compressed the verification space). At the point of failure, the program had no mitigation capacity — the tank geometry was fixed, the material system was fixed, and the test was required for program progression.

**Prediction: PRESENT HIGH**

---

### WSP — ABSENT | HIGH

The X-33 composite tank failure is not characterized by a pattern of individually sub-threshold anomalies accumulating across time. The failure mechanism (delamination under combined cryogenic loading) was a material behavior that was not observable in the pre-test signal environment. There is no documented pattern of composite anomaly signals that were individually managed without aggregation.

**Prediction: ABSENT HIGH**

---

### HCL — ABSENT | HIGH

The failure involves a single technical system (composite tank) within a single program organizational structure. It does not exhibit the cross-domain independent source structure that HCL requires. The materials team and structural design team are not structurally independent domains with correlated anomalous signals sharing a hidden structural source — they are sequential actors in a design process with an interface verification gap.

**Prediction: ABSENT HIGH**

---

### CDA — PRESENT | MODERATE

The cryogenic material characterization data did not fully cross the boundary from the materials domain into the structural design decision context in its most critical form — the specific implications of microcracking and delamination risk under combined loading at the X-33 tank geometry were not admitted into the design acceptance decision. This is a weaker CDA signal than SI-2 because the general cryogenic concern was present in the program's awareness; what was not admitted was the specific combined-loading implication.

**Prediction: PRESENT MODERATE**

---

### FA — ABSENT | HIGH

The failure is attributable to a specific material-system boundary verification gap, not to multiple independently managed safety margins simultaneously degraded.

**Prediction: ABSENT HIGH**

---

### Conventional Explanation Score Prediction: LOW (0.25–0.40)

The conventional account: "composite material failed under cryogenic conditions; the technology was not mature enough." The EE/CIS structural account adds: the specific mechanism by which the technology risk was transferred (LD) and the specific boundary verification gap (SI-2) that made the failure possible within an otherwise rigorous engineering program. The CES is low but not as low as T1-001 — the X-33 failure is less narratively saturated and the structural account adds more beyond the conventional explanation.

## Case T2-007 — Air France Flight 447

**CASE-ID:** T2-007  
**Tier:** 2 | **AI Prior Exposure:** MEDIUM  
**Outcome:** Air France Flight 447, an Airbus A330-203, crashed into the Atlantic Ocean on June 1, 2009, killing all 228 aboard. The Bureau d'Enquêtes et d'Analyses (BEA) determined the cause was: temporary inconsistency of airspeed indications following pitot tube icing, leading to autopilot disconnection; followed by inappropriate crew inputs causing the aircraft to stall; sustained stall through the 4.5-minute descent to impact.

**Phase Boundaries:**
- Pre-Decision Phase start: First documented pitot tube icing event affecting A330/A340 airspeed indication, approximately 2003–2004
- Pre-Decision Phase end: AF447 departure from Rio de Janeiro, June 1, 2009, 19:29 UTC
- Decision Phase: Loss of reliable airspeed indication through crew response, approximately 02:10–02:14 UTC June 2
- Post-Decision Phase end: Ocean impact, June 2, 2009, approximately 02:14 UTC

**Falsification conditions stated before reconstruction:**
- SI-3 fails if BEA reconstruction shows that pitot icing events across the A330/A340 fleet were formally aggregated into a systemic risk assessment before AF447 (assessment may have been incorrect, but aggregation must have been absent for SI-3 to be confirmed)
- SI-4 fails if the simultaneous conflicting signals during the descent are reducible to a single-cause cascade (pitot icing → everything else) rather than constituting genuinely multi-dimensional structural incongruence in the signal environment
- CR fails if the crew's response to the stall warning does not constitute a documented closure of a contradicting signal

---

### CR — PRESENT | HIGH

During the 4.5-minute descent, the stall warning activated multiple times. The stall warning is a contradicting signal: it directly asserts that the aircraft is in a stall, which is incompatible with the PF's (pilot flying's) understanding of the aircraft state (the PF believed the aircraft was in a high-speed condition, not a stall). The crew's response — maintaining nose-up inputs despite the stall warning — constitutes closure of the contradiction by alternative explanation: "the stall warning is erroneous given our believed aircraft state." The closure was not based on direct disconfirmation of the stall warning. The stall warning was correct; the aircraft was stalling. Options Released: the correct diagnosis that the aircraft was in a stall, and the corresponding recovery inputs.

**Prediction: PRESENT HIGH**

---

### SI — PRESENT | HIGH | SI-3 and SI-4

**SI-3 (Temporal accumulation):** From approximately 2003 through 2009, multiple Air France A330/A340 aircraft experienced temporary loss of airspeed indication due to pitot tube icing in high-altitude convective conditions. Each event was addressed individually — airworthiness directives were issued, service bulletins were generated, pitot tube upgrades were recommended but not mandated. No formal aggregate pattern assessment connected the individual events into a systemic operational risk requiring mandatory fleet-wide pitot tube replacement or mandatory operational procedure change before AF447. The aggregate pattern — repeated temporary airspeed loss events in the same aircraft type, in the same atmospheric conditions, across multiple operators and years — was present in the data but not formally assessed as a pattern prior to AF447.

**SI-4 (Configuration incongruence):** During the 4.5-minute descent, the aircraft's signal environment exhibited simultaneous multi-dimensional incongruence with any valid classified flight state. Simultaneously: unreliable airspeed from multiple pitot-static probes; stall warning oscillating between active and inactive (driven by airspeed validity logic); high altitude with reduced stall margin; night instrument meteorological conditions; gross motor inputs from PF inconsistent with the aircraft's actual attitude; attitude information available but not cross-referenced against pitch and power. No single classified flight state accounts for this configuration. A normal cruise state predicts reliable airspeed and inactive stall warning. A stall state predicts active stall warning and consistent recovery inputs. A high-speed upset state predicts unreliable airspeed without stall warning. The actual signal configuration fit none of these states — it was structurally incongruent with any classified state.

**Predictions: SI-3 PRESENT HIGH | SI-4 PRESENT HIGH**

Both are falsifiable at HIGH confidence. SI-3 fails if formal fleet aggregation existed. SI-4 fails if the multi-dimensional incongruence reduces to a single pitot-cause cascade.

---

### WSP — PRESENT | HIGH

The individual pitot icing events across the A330/A340 fleet constitute the WSP signal set. Each event was below the threshold for mandatory operational change — each was addressed as an isolated airworthiness matter. The aggregate of events across the fleet and across years constituted a pattern that, if formally aggregated, would have crossed the threshold for mandatory fleet-wide intervention. WSP is confirmed at HIGH confidence because the BEA specifically documented the history of pitot icing events and the absence of mandatory action as a contributing factor to AF447.

**Prediction: PRESENT HIGH**

Note: WSP and SI-3 are co-present. WSP identifies the governance failure (no aggregation mechanism). SI-3 identifies the structural property of the observation that escaped detection (temporal accumulation across periods). They are related but distinct codes.

---

### LD — ABSENT | HIGH

The AF447 failure is a systemic aviation safety failure and a human factors failure. It does not exhibit the organizational risk transfer structure that LD requires. There is no identifiable moment at which risk was transferred from a domain with evaluation capacity to a domain without it. The flight crew had the instruments, the training, and in principle the capacity to recognize and respond to the stall — the failure is misinterpretation under stress, not displacement of risk to an evaluation-incapable context.

**Prediction: ABSENT HIGH**

---

### HCL — ABSENT | HIGH

AF447 is a single-flight event. The failure does not involve structurally independent domains with correlated anomalous signals sharing a hidden common source. The simultaneous signal failures are within a single cockpit, attributable to a single root cause (pitot icing). HCL is absent.

**Prediction: ABSENT HIGH**

---

### CDA — ABSENT | HIGH

All relevant signals were present in the cockpit. The failure is not that information from an adjacent domain failed to cross a boundary — it is that the crew could not correctly interpret the signals that were already present. Admission failure is not the mechanism. Interpretation failure under novel, high-stress conditions is.

**Prediction: ABSENT HIGH**

---

### FA — PRESENT | HIGH

Multiple simultaneously degraded conditions during the flight: pitot tube icing creating unreliable airspeed; night instrument meteorological conditions (no visual reference); cruise altitude with reduced aerodynamic margins; PF with limited A330 command experience; co-pilots out of briefing rhythm with primary captain (who was on rest); reduced cognitive bandwidth from the unusual situation. No single condition would have caused the accident. The simultaneous co-occurrence of all these degraded conditions created cumulative vulnerability that no individual condition's assessment would reveal. The BEA report explicitly analyzes the combination of conditions as a contributing factor set.

**Prediction: PRESENT HIGH**

---

### Conventional Explanation Score Prediction: MEDIUM (0.45–0.60)

The conventional account: "pitot tubes iced over, autopilot disconnected, pilots stalled the plane." The EE/CIS structural account adds: SI-3 (fleet-wide pitot events not aggregated), SI-4 (multi-dimensional signal incongruence during descent), CR (stall warning closed by misidentification), WSP (fleet event pattern not formally assessed), FA (simultaneous condition accumulation). The CES is medium — the conventional account covers the proximate cause; the structural account adds the pre-flight systemic failures and the in-flight signal interpretation structure.

## Case T2-008 — Überlingen Mid-Air Collision

**CASE-ID:** T2-008  
**Tier:** 2 | **AI Prior Exposure:** MEDIUM-LOW  
**Outcome:** On July 1, 2002, Bashkirian Airlines Flight 2937 (Tu-154) and DHL Flight 611 (B757) collided over Überlingen, Germany, at 35,000 feet, killing all 71 aboard both aircraft. The German BFU investigation determined that the Bashkirian crew followed an ATC instruction to descend rather than the TCAS Resolution Advisory (RA) to climb, and both aircraft descended into each other.

**Phase Boundaries:**
- Pre-Decision Phase start: Operational introduction of TCAS II and establishment of TCAS-ATC priority procedures in European airspace — approximately 1993–1995
- Pre-Decision Phase end: TCAS RA issued to Bashkirian Flight 2937, approximately 21:35:04 UTC July 1, 2002
- Decision Phase: Bashkirian crew response to simultaneous TCAS RA and ATC instruction, approximately 21:35:04–21:35:32 UTC
- Post-Decision Phase end: Mid-air collision, July 1, 2002, approximately 21:35:32 UTC

**Falsification conditions stated before reconstruction:**
- SI-2 fails if the BFU reconstruction shows that the TCAS-ATC priority interface was structurally enforced through crew training and procedures in a way that makes the failure individual non-compliance rather than interface coupling failure
- CR is predicted as PRIMARY — if SI-2 fails, CR becomes the sole primary construct

---

### CR — PRESENT | HIGH

The TCAS Resolution Advisory issued to Bashkirian Flight 2937 at 21:35:04 UTC is a contradicting signal. It directly contradicts the ATC instruction (issued approximately 21:34:42 UTC) to descend. The crew resolved the contradiction by following the ATC instruction and descending. This constitutes closure of the TCAS RA by alternative explanation adoption: "ATC knows our situation; we should follow ATC." The TCAS RA was the correct guidance; following it would have resulted in separation. The ATC instruction was given without awareness of the DHL aircraft's position (the controller was on the phone, the secondary radar had a technical issue). Options Released: the separation that would have resulted from following the TCAS RA.

**Prediction: PRESENT HIGH**

CR is the most strongly supported prediction in this case regardless of SI-2 outcome.

---

### SI — PRESENT | MODERATE | SI-2

**This is the adversarial prediction.** The TCAS RA constitutes information that must cross the automation-human decision boundary to be actionable. The structural question is whether the interface coupling at this boundary — from TCAS output to crew action, in the presence of a simultaneous contradicting ATC instruction — was adequate. The ICAO PANS-ATM and operational procedures established that TCAS RAs take priority over ATC instructions. This priority rule existed in documentation and training. However, the actual interface coupling at the moment of conflict was not structurally enforced — it depended on crew recognition and active choice to follow TCAS over ATC. The interface coupling failed: the information (TCAS RA) crossed the boundary but was not admitted in a form that governed crew action.

**This is the falsifiable prediction.** If the BFU reconstruction shows that the TCAS-ATC priority rule was clearly established in crew training, procedures, and recurrent training — making the Bashkirian crew's failure individual non-compliance with a known rule rather than an interface coupling failure — then SI-2 is absent. The failure would then be CR only (known procedure contradicted by ATC, closed by following ATC). The reconstruction must adjudicate between these framings.

**Prediction: PRESENT MODERATE | Sub-criterion: SI-2**

Confidence is MODERATE rather than HIGH specifically because the adversarial case against SI-2 is strong: the TCAS priority rule was documented and trained. If the crew knew the rule and chose not to follow it, this is CR without SI-2.

---

### LD — ABSENT | HIGH

The failure does not involve organizational risk transfer. The Bashkirian crew received all relevant information in the cockpit. The failure is not that a receiving context lacked evaluation capacity — it is that the crew resolved a real-time conflict in the wrong direction.

**Prediction: ABSENT HIGH**

---

### WSP — ABSENT | HIGH

No documented sub-threshold signal accumulation pattern. The Überlingen collision is a single-event decision failure, not a pattern accumulation failure.

**Prediction: ABSENT HIGH**

---

### HCL — ABSENT | HIGH

The collision involves two aircraft in direct proximity, not independent domains with a hidden common structural source. Both aircraft received TCAS advisories (complementary RAs). The Bashkirian crew's advisory and the DHL crew's advisory are related by design (TCAS coordinates). The failure is not a hidden integration failure — it is a known conflict resolved incorrectly by one crew.

**Prediction: ABSENT HIGH**

---

### CDA — ABSENT | HIGH

The TCAS RA was received in the cockpit. The ATC instruction was received in the cockpit. Both signals were admitted. The failure is resolution, not admission.

**Prediction: ABSENT HIGH**

---

### FA — PRESENT | MODERATE

Multiple simultaneously degraded operational conditions: single controller managing multiple sectors (staffing); primary radar showing technical issue; controller on telephone during the conflict resolution period; Bashkirian crew unfamiliarity with TCAS (TCAS was relatively new to Russian carrier operations); night operations; both aircraft in same airspace block simultaneously. No single condition caused the collision. The simultaneous co-occurrence created a vulnerability environment that contributed to the outcome.

**Prediction: PRESENT MODERATE**

---

### Conventional Explanation Score Prediction: MEDIUM-HIGH (0.55–0.70)

The conventional account: "crew followed ATC instead of TCAS; ATC error; TCAS priority rule not followed." The EE/CIS structural account adds: the SI-2 question (whether the TCAS-ATC interface coupling was structurally adequate), CR (the specific closure mechanism), FA (the simultaneous degraded conditions). The CES is medium-high because the conventional account already identifies the TCAS vs. ATC conflict as the mechanism. The EE/CIS account's marginal contribution is the structural framing of how the conflict resolution mechanism failed.
